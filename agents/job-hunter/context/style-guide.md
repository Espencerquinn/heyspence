# Job-Hunter Style Guide & Fit Rubric

This governs how the agent judges fit and drafts materials for Spencer Quinn.

## Who Spencer is (positioning)
A founder/operator who is genuinely AI-native: 15+ years scaling revenue orgs to $80M+, full P&L
ownership, GTM strategy & operations — AND hands-on building with Claude Code, the Anthropic API, and
agentic/multi-agent systems. He is fluent in Spanish, has an EMBA (Strategy) and an MIT AI-product cert,
and a full-stack dev background. He sits at the intersection of **commercial leadership** and **technical
builder**, which lets him credibly target two role families.

## What Spencer is looking for (search scope)
Two tracks, both in play:
1. **Anthropic full-time roles** — his original target; sourced from Anthropic's Greenhouse board.
2. **Fractional / part-time / contract / advisory roles** — broader market (any company), e.g.
   fractional CMO/COO/Head of GTM/RevOps, fractional AI lead/advisor, part-time operator, AI consultant.

**Preference order (higher = more desirable):**
fractional/part-time **in Utah** > fractional/part-time **remote** > Anthropic full-time (strong-fit) >
other full-time. Encode this as a preference *bump*, not a hard filter — a great Anthropic full-time role
still scores well.

## Fit scoring (0–100)
Score each role against Spencer's profile. Base rubric:
- **85–100 — Strong fit.** Role centers on his proven strengths: GTM strategy/ops, revenue ops,
  sales/partnerships, startup programs, bizops/chief-of-staff, solutions/forward-deployed, AI
  product/program/advisory roles where builder+commercial blend wins. Seniority plausible (manager →
  director → lead/head, or fractional equivalent).
- **60–84 — Plausible fit.** Adjacent or a stretch in seniority/domain, but his story can be told convincingly. Worth drafting.
- **40–59 — Weak fit.** Tangential, or seniority/domain mismatch he can't credibly bridge. Do NOT draft; archive with a one-line reason.
- **0–39 — No fit.** Deep technical IC research/engineering roles (ML research, systems eng), or unrelated functions. Archive, no draft.

**Preference adjustments (apply after the base score, cap at 100):**
- Fractional / part-time / contract / advisory engagement: **+10**.
- Based in Utah (or hybrid/onsite in Utah): **+10**. Remote-US: neutral.
- Non-US-only location for a full-time role (e.g. London/Dublin/Sydney-only, no remote): **−15**.

**Only draft when fit_score ≥ 60.** Below that, set status `Archived` and write a short `fit_summary` explaining why.

### Location
Spencer is in Utah (Mountain Time). Utah-based and remote-US roles are both workable; Utah is preferred
(see the +10 above). Always note the location and engagement type (FT vs fractional/part-time) in `fit_summary`.

## Which voice to use
- **Commercial / GTM / partnerships / sales / startup-programs / bizops** → mirror `example-startup-partnerships.md`.
- **Developer-facing / solutions / forward-deployed / enablement / DevRel / technical-program** → mirror `example-claude-evangelist.md`.
- **Mixed** → blend, leading with whichever the JD weights more.

## Résumé drafting rules
- One page. Lead with a 2–3 line summary retargeted to the role's exact title and themes.
- Reorder/curate bullets to surface the most relevant experience first; reuse real bullets from the
  master résumé and the two examples. **Never invent employers, titles, dates, degrees, or metrics.**
- Mirror the JD's vocabulary where it's truthful (e.g., if they say "forward-deployed," use it if apt).
- Keep Spanish, MIT AI cert, EMBA, and Claude Code/agentic builds visible — they're differentiators.
- Output as clean Markdown.

## Cover-letter drafting rules
- ~250–350 words, 3–4 short paragraphs, first person, warm but crisp — not effusive.
- Open with a specific hook tying Spencer to *this* role/team and to Anthropic's mission
  (reliable, interpretable, steerable AI; safety + beneficial deployment).
- Middle: 2–3 concrete proof points from his history that map to the role's needs (quantified).
- Close: enthusiasm + clear, low-pressure call to talk. Sign "Spencer Quinn."
- No clichés ("I am writing to apply…"), no invented facts, no over-claiming seniority.

## fit_summary
2–3 sentences: why it fits (or doesn't), the angle/voice chosen, and any caveat (location, seniority).

## Integrity guardrails
- Everything must be defensible in an interview. When unsure of a fact, omit it.
- If two source variants disagree on a number, use the master résumé's figure.
